Holger Hoos (Leiden University, NL) (Mar 12 2020 at 17:01):

I am delighted to let you know that we have received official notifications that the results of the ICT-48 selection process look amazingly good for CLAIRE:

Holger Hoos (Leiden University, NL) (Mar 12 2020 at 17:02):

Our CSA proposal, VISION, has been selected for funding, along with the TAILOR and HumanE-AI-Net proposals in which CLAIRE had major involvement.

Holger Hoos (Leiden University, NL) (Mar 12 2020 at 17:03):

Overall, four of the five ICT-48 proposals selected for funding are coordinated by members of CLAIRE.

Holger Hoos (Leiden University, NL) (Mar 12 2020 at 17:05):

At the same time, the ICT-48 process has produced an outcome that does not directly benefit important parts of the European AI research ecosystem. It is unfortunate that the call and process have been structured in a way that produces winners and loser. VISION, TAILOR and HumanE-AI have all been constructed in a way that brings benefits also to institutions and groups outside of the respective consortia.

Holger Hoos (Leiden University, NL) (Mar 12 2020 at 17:08):

CLAIRE is deeply committed to working with the European Commission to ensure that those critical parts of the European AI research landscape that are not directly covered by winning ICT-48 proposals will be supported in future rounds of funding. We need to ensure that all AI research excellence in Europe is leveraged towards the success of "AI made in Europe", as per the CLAIRE vision.
