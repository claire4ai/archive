Alexa Kodde (CLAIRE Headquarters, NL) (Oct 26 2020 at 09:03):

DSEG Decision Tree Workshop
Managing the Risks and Realizing the Rewards of Humanitarian Data Science

Thursday, 5 November
12:00 London GMT, 20:00 Manila PST, 13:00 Geneva CET, 07:00 Boston EST

Meeting link here

Data Science Ethics Group (DSEG) are pleased to invite you to the launch of the Data Science Ethics Group (DSEG) Decision Tree for the Ethical use of Advanced Data Science Methods in the Humanitarian Sector. This interactive online tool guides humanitarians through five critical steps of ethical project design. The Tree, which is an outgrowth of DSEG’s Ethical Framework, allows users to take advantage of emerging technologies with the confidence that they have considered and mitigated biases and potential harm.

DSEG will be joined by experts in the fields of AI ethics, innovation and human rights for a panel discussion on the current state and future of the field:

  • Virginia Dignum
    Wallenberg Chair, Professor of Responsible Artificial Intelligence at Umeå Universitet

  • Adam Lang
    Head of Nesta Scotland

  • Cecilia Jimenez Damary
    Special Rapporteur on Human Rights of IDPs

Itinerary:
12:00 Welcome Remarks: Jeffrey Labovitz, IOM
12:10 Introduction to DSEG and the Decision Tree
12:20 Interactive Decision Tree Demonstration
12:40 Panel Discussion
13:25 Concluding Remarks

DSEG Ethical Framework | Humanitarian Data Science and Ethics Group
This is a framework for applying data science methods for humanitarian outcomes. It aims to provide a set of ethical and practical guidelines for humanitarian data collectors, users, and stakeholders to consider when applying data science for humanitarian work. This work is at the juncture of data science (in particular AI), ethics, responsible data management, humanitarian innovation, and humanitarian principles and standards.
