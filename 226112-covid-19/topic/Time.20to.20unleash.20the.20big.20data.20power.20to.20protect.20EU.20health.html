Gianluca Bontempi (ULB, Brussels, B) (Mar 13 2020 at 21:20):

Thanks Holger for this thread.
Now that, after several hesitations, several EU states (including France and Belgium) took restrictive actions, the economy will necessarily slow down. However let us consider that EU will keep producing the most important resource to understand this disease and help protect our health system: DATA. You probably heard already of Chinese predictive systems to distinguish conventional from covid pneumonia from x-ray imaging but several other tasks may be imagined to help protecting EU health system:
1) use of causal inference to assess the impact of restrictive actions on epidemics
2) predict the probability of positive slabs from observable features (to prioritize the treatment)
3) predict the risk of positive patients to prioritize the access to IC.

I think that CLAIRE is a great opportunity to join forces against our common enemy. Please use this thread to share ideas, data, skills, resources and demands for collaboration/support. Several scientists are ready to help. Stay smart! Stay safe!

Hans De Canck (Mar 17 2020 at 11:13):

Hi all, several initiatives are shaping up, initiated by established network organisations. I believe, in line with the call of Gianluca, it is worthwhile to mention here the initiative of GO-FAIR: the Virus Outbreak Data Network (VODAN).
More info you can find here: https://www.go-fair.org/implementation-networks/overview/vodan/
It seems a good way forward to link up to this initiative, push forward this approach to create FAIR (in this sense also meaning Federated, AI–Ready) data where the CLAIRE community can work with in contributing to the search for adequate solutions in the field of healthcare & prevention. In its turn, these sets can be linked with other (non-clinical) data sets to look for potential AI driven solutions in logistics and planning and communication actions (fake news!) as well
