Topic: Invitation to join spotGEO, the latest competition from ESA.

Dario Izzo (European Space Agency, NL) (May 29 2020 at 14:58):

Hi @all ,

At the European Space Agency, from the Advanced Concepts Team and we have just finalized the spotGEO competition dataset which we are going to release publicly as a competition for ML and AI.

The competition will run from the 8th of June to th end of August.

In the "Spot the GEO satellites" challenge, or spotGEO challenge, participants will be asked to develop an AI to detect Geostationary orbiting objects from simple png images (or frames) acquired by an unkown, low-cost ground-based telescope. Can AI approaches cope with cloud cover, atmospheric/weather effects, light pollution, sensor noise/defects, star occlusions and more?

Have a look at the Competition pages and read also the official ESA news.

Telescope-peering AI challenged to spot mystery space objects
ESA’s latest public competition challenges ‘citizen scientists’ to combine AI with observations from low-cost telescopes to pick out mystery objects in and around geostationary orbit, thousands of kilometres above Earth.
