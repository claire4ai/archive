Jos van der Wijst (Oct 10 2020 at 16:10):

Legal AIR
We (my law firm BG.legal) are developing a platform with information on the legal and ethical aspect of AI (Legal AI Rules & Regulation). Information on legal and ethical aspects of the use of AI is widely available (on the internet) and often written by lawyers and for lawyers. The translation into practice is lacking. In addition, there is such an amount of sources and information, that it is difficult to find it and to understand. Certainly for (start-ups, scale-ups and SMEs) companies and governments. They struggle with practical questions such as who owns the data, who owns the model, who owns the algorithm, who is liable for AI-based decisions, etc. But also for individuals looking for information about their rights in the field of AI applications such as facial recognition, decisions based on an algorithm, etc.

There is a need for a practical translation of laws and regulations (which rules apply, model clauses, codes of conduct), insight into the phasing of AI projects in order to identify and mitigate (ethical and legal) risks, knowledge about data governance, data mining, privacy and compliance, contacts with experts who can provide these answers (drafting ethical codes of conduct, audits compliance codes of conduct), events to be updated / trained, FAQ, etc. This practical translation must be made available to citizens, governments and companies.

We work together with knowledge institutions (Universities, civil societies), companies and governments to set up this platform.

We are looking for similar initiatives in Europe. Who knows about such initiatives? If these are not (yet) available, we want to take this platform to a European level. We are looking for partners and funding for this. Who has suggestions for this? Please contact me (wijst@bg.legal).
