Gregg Barrett (Cirrus) (Oct 17 2019 at 08:03):

Dave, I can't speak to CLAIRE's intentions on this matter but I was wondering if you have engaged with the Hartree Centre for the use of their compute platform?
At present we are working on a compute platform to serve all the research efforts for universities in Africa, and access will be extended to include collaborations with universities and international entities beyond Africa. In this regard, I look forward to CLAIRE participation/collaboration. The collaboration framework agreement is being drafted by Wits University and I have asked Wits to include CLAIRE as a potential participant.
I am happy to include your university as well, as well as any other interested university - as we see this as a way to significantly bolster international collaboration between Africa and the rest of the world.

Hartree Centre: https://www.hartree.stfc.ac.uk
Wits University: https://www.wits.ac.za

Gregg Barrett (Cirrus) (Nov 15 2019 at 12:16):

@Dave Howcroft (Heriot-Watt University, UK) The consortium under discussion pertains to Africa's AI initiative - which includes participation of universities and research entities from all around the world. So the consortium is separate to CLAIRE but the objective is to support and collaborate with CLAIRE as best we can.
Concerning participation (which I am going to raise with the CLAIRE team) it could be done through CLAIRE (if the legal structure permits) and then all those participating in CLAIRE will be included, or we could work with CLAIRE and have individual universities and research entities that are participating in CLAIRE each join the consortium individually.
In both instances the objective is the same - to make our resources available and foster collaboration.
