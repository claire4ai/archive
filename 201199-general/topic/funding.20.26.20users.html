Basile Starynkevitch (Feb 10 2021 at 17:40):

Hello
With other people, I am developing (informally) the RefPerSys open source symbolic artificial intelligence system (GPLv3+ licensed, for Linux onlypartn)
see http://refpersys.org/ for details.
The major ideas are from Jacques Pitrat (1934-2019) who was the director of my PhD jury, defended in 1990. See Pitrat's blog still on http://bootstrappingartificialintelligence.fr/WordPress3/ and for those speaking French, https://afia.asso.fr/journee-hommage-j-pitrat/

They include:
meta-programming: RefPerSys is generating some C++ code, and more and more of it. The long term goal is to generate all the C++ code of RefPerSys, and take advantage of existing Linux open source software components and tools (the GCC compiler, libbacktrace, libonion, ....)

frame-based programming. RefPerSys is keeping "knowledge" in objects similar to frames.

persistence: RefPerSys is a persistent system, capable of storing most of its state in disk files or databases. The idea is to generate the C++ code managing that persistence, and the objects related to that persistence generating that C++ code are themselves persistent.

rule-based system: RefPerSys will have rules and metarules generating C++ code. The goal is that RefPerSys becomes some expert system like.

My major problem in RefPerSys is to get some funding and partners interested in it in Europe. (I have partners contributing to it from India).

So I am seeking some partners and some funding for RefPerSys.

I am employed at CEA LIST in France near Paris, my professional email is <basile.starynkevitch@cea.fr> and my private email is <basile@starynkevitch.net>

I am not a theoretician, more a software developer (very fund of free software) than a computer scientist.

My employer is mostly asking me for funding. Perhaps some HorizonEurope call could fund RefPerSys.... But I don't know which one.

Non goals include: interacting with Windows or MacOSX computers directly. Any such interaction will go thru the Web (so the computer running Windows or MacOSX is just a web browser).
Having to use Windows or MicroSoft Word. I never used Windows computers in my life. I am reasonably fluent with Linux (using it since 1993) and Unix (using it since 1985).
