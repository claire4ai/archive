Vít Dočkal (CLAIRE Office Prague, CIIRC CTU) (Mar 14 2020 at 14:32):

Now we have a prototype of the respirator printed on a 3D printer with MJF technology. First tests are very promising and have shown minor improvements to be done. After the final prototype is verified we will focus on finding a way how to ensure mass production. One possibility is to design a form for the injection molding process. We will appreciate anyone who is experienced in designing the injection molding forms to discuss the requirements needed for a proper form to be produced. We expect the form could be also produced with additive manufacturing.The weight is 35g. With filter it will be around 50g. It fits well and seems to be well sealed.
It seems we are going a good direction.

Emanuela Girardi (Mar 17 2020 at 20:45):

CLAIRE is working to collect the “AI & Covid-19” projects and efforts in Europe and to build and organise a network of AI experts and volunteers to work on these projects.

AI technologies can be a great support in fighting the corona virus, by working together, we can show the potential of AI, create a positive impact and ultimately share the best practices within the EU and the world.

Information on how to participate are coming soon.

Here is an interesting initiative.

The Allen Institute for AI has partnered with leading research groups to prepare and distribute the COVID-19 Open Research Dataset (CORD-19), a free resource of over 29,000 scholarly articles, including over 13,000 with full text, about COVID-19 and the coronavirus family of viruses for use by the global research community.
This dataset is intended to mobilize researchers to apply recent advances in natural language processing to generate new insights in support of the fight against this infectious disease. The corpus will be updated weekly as new research is published in peer-reviewed publications and archival services like bioRxiv, medRxiv, and others.
Please see: https://pages.semanticscholar.org/coronavirus-research for background, a free corpus, and a Kaggle competition.

Emanuela Girardi (Mar 23 2020 at 11:11):

Safe Paths
Safe Paths is an app that tells you if you have crossed paths with somebody who is infected, it shares information about your movements in a privacy-preserving way—and could let health officials tackle coronavirus hot spots. It is the “privacy compliant” version of the Chinese and South Korean app to track the movements of infected people.
It is a free and open-source app and it has been developed by a team at MIT Media Lab and Harvard.
https://www.technologyreview.com/s/615372/coronavirus-infection-tests-app-pandemic-location-privacy/
http://safepaths.mit.edu/
